Life Story

Information Systems Since 2002

I remember when we got our first computer. It was a used computer by the time we got it for Christmas. It was DOS based and used the 5 1/4" floppy disks. Mostly we used it for playing games.

A few years later, my parents purchased a computer running Win 95. There are photos of me on that computer. My sister once said, "Oh look, you were a nerd, even back then!" I wasn't offended.

Education

When I was in High School, I was taking computer classes at the local Technical School. As a senior, the school teacher over the Sterling Scholar assigned me the History Sterling Scholar representative. The girl they assigned over the Information Systems had no idea about computers. I went to her and asked her to switch. She readily agreed and I was the Information Systems Sterling Scholar for my High School. At the competition, I actually placed as a runner-up!

In college, I earned an associates degree in Network Management at LDS Business College. I worked with the student Organization SIFE and competed at Nationals. For our project, we refurbished donated computers and gave them to low-income children and schools.

Career

I began working at a residential treatment center as their Network administrator. I designed and built a campus with network infrastructure and over 50 laptops and desktops. I did everything from running fiber between buildings to fixing users password and printing problems. I connected seven buildings and one main building that had classrooms and a computer lab.

After the treatment center, I worked as the IT Director for Duchesne County. I was responsible for maintaining a network of over 200 devices and a huge geographic area. The county jail was a major responsibility as they required 24/7 connectivity. During this time, I completely overhauled the outdated website and upgraded the email system to gmail.

Currently, I am the State of Utah, where I have been since 2011. I service all State agencies from Tabiona to Whiterocks Utah. These agencies include DWS, DCFS, DHS, AP&P, Corrections, UHP, DLD, DMV, DNR, and Agriculture. My responsibilities include everything from deploying and surplussing equipment to unlocking accounts. I am also always trying to find the best solution for the employees to make their job easier and more efficient. I always work to know my fellow employees and provide service that they can trust.
